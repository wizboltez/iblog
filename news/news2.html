AirTag 2 Announced

Apple's next-generation tracking device with improved features

Apple Unveils AirTag 2: Smarter, Louder, Better

Apple has officially announced the second generation of its popular item tracker, AirTag 2, during a special event at Apple Park. The new AirTag 2 builds upon the success of the original with significant improvements to tracking accuracy, audio alerts, battery life, and design.

The AirTag 2 features an improved speaker system that produces significantly louder alerts, making it easier to locate items when they're hidden under cushions, inside bags, or in other muffled environments. Apple has also enhanced the Ultra Wideband chip for more precise directional finding and increased the effective range when used with the Find My network.

"AirTag has become an essential tool for millions of customers to keep track of their important items," said Apple's VP of Worldwide Product Marketing. "With AirTag 2, we've made it even better with a louder speaker, improved tracking accuracy, and a more environmentally friendly design‚Äîall while maintaining the same seamless experience our users love."

Key Improvements

AirTag 2 introduces several notable improvements while maintaining the same compact disc-shaped design that users are familiar with. The device now features a more robust water and dust resistance rating of IP68 (up from IP67), allowing it to withstand submersion in deeper water for longer periods.

One of the most requested features, a louder speaker, has been implemented in AirTag 2. The new speaker system is up to twice as loud as the original, making it much easier to locate items in noisy environments or when the AirTag is inside a bag or drawer.

Apple has also enhanced the privacy features, with improved anti-stalking protections that can alert users more quickly if an unknown AirTag appears to be traveling with them. The companion iOS app has been updated with a more intuitive interface and additional customization options.

Environmental Impact

Apple continues its commitment to environmental responsibility with the AirTag 2. The new tracker uses 100% recycled aluminum in its enclosure, and the packaging is made from 100% fiber-based materials, supporting Apple's goal to eliminate plastic from all packaging by 2025.

The company also announced an AirTag recycling program, offering customers a discount on new AirTag 2 purchases when they trade in their original AirTags, ensuring the materials are properly recovered and reused.
