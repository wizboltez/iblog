iPad Pro M3 Launched: A New Era of Professional Computing

Published: April 20, 2025 By: Apple News Category: Product Launch

Apple has officially unveiled the new iPad Pro powered by the M3 chip, marking a significant leap forward in tablet computing performance. The latest professional-grade tablet from Apple introduces a stunning OLED display, enhanced performance capabilities, and new features aimed at creative professionals and power users.

Revolutionary M3 Chip Performance

At the heart of the new iPad Pro is Apple's M3 chip, bringing desktop-class performance to the tablet form factor. The M3 chip features an 8-core CPU with 4 performance cores and 4 efficiency cores, as well as a 10-core GPU that delivers up to 30% faster graphics performance compared to the previous generation.

The Neural Engine in the M3 chip has been significantly upgraded with 16 cores, enabling faster machine learning tasks and enhanced AI capabilities. This allows for improved photo and video processing, real-time filters, and more sophisticated augmented reality experiences.

"The M3 chip transforms iPad Pro into a computing powerhouse that outperforms most laptops while maintaining the versatility and portability of a tablet," said John Ternus, Apple's Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering. "Professional users can now accomplish complex tasks that previously required a high-end desktop computer."

Breakthrough OLED Display

For the first time in an iPad, Apple has incorporated OLED technology into the display, which they're calling the "Liquid Retina XDR OLED" display. This premium display offers perfect blacks, improved contrast ratios, and more vibrant colors than ever before.

The 11-inch and 12.9-inch models feature resolutions of 2388 x 1668 and 2732 x 2048 pixels respectively, with a peak brightness of 1600 nits for HDR content. The ProMotion technology enables adaptive refresh rates up to 120Hz, ensuring smooth scrolling and responsive touch input.

The new display also supports P3 wide color gamut, True Tone technology, and has an anti-reflective coating that reduces glare by 25% compared to previous models, making it ideal for outdoor use.

Professional Camera System

The iPad Pro M3 features a pro camera system with a 12MP Wide camera, a 10MP Ultra Wide camera, and LiDAR Scanner. The cameras leverage the computational photography capabilities of the M3 chip, enabling features like Smart HDR 4, Deep Fusion, and Apple ProRAW support.

Video capabilities have been enhanced with the ability to record 4K video at 60fps with extended dynamic range. The front-facing camera has been upgraded to a 12MP TrueDepth camera with Center Stage, which automatically keeps you in frame during video calls.

Connectivity and Accessories

The new iPad Pro includes Thunderbolt 4 connectivity, allowing for faster data transfers and support for high-resolution external displays. Wi-Fi 6E and optional 5G cellular connectivity ensure you stay connected with the fastest wireless technologies available.

Apple also introduced the new Magic Keyboard designed specifically for the iPad Pro M3, featuring a floating cantilever design, backlit keys, and a built-in trackpad. The keyboard now includes a row of function keys for quick access to common tasks.

Alongside the new iPad Pro, Apple announced the Apple Pencil Pro, which adds haptic feedback, gesture controls, and a new "squeeze" interaction that allows you to quickly switch between tools or bring up a color palette.

iPadOS 19 Integration

The iPad Pro M3 comes with iPadOS 19 pre-installed, which brings several new features designed specifically for the pro user. The operating system includes enhanced multitasking capabilities, allowing users to run up to four apps simultaneously with the new "Quad View" feature.

Professional apps have been updated to take advantage of the M3 chip's performance. Adobe has optimized their Creative Cloud suite for the new iPad Pro, enabling desktop-class photo and video editing. Apple's own professional apps, including Final Cut Pro and Logic Pro, have also been updated with new features that leverage the M3 chip's capabilities.

iPadOS 19 also introduces "Pro Workflows," a new feature that allows users to create and save custom configurations of apps and windows for different tasks, making it easier to switch between different projects.

Environmental Impact

Apple continues its commitment to the environment with the iPad Pro M3. The device uses 100% recycled aluminum in the enclosure, 100% recycled tin in the solder of the main logic board, and 100% recycled rare earth elements in all magnets.

The packaging is made from 100% recycled and responsible sources, and Apple is committed to being carbon neutral for its entire product footprint by 2030.

Pricing and Availability

The new iPad Pro M3 starts at $799 for the 11-inch model with 128GB storage and Wi-Fi, and $1,099 for the 12.9-inch model with 128GB storage and Wi-Fi. Models with cellular connectivity start at $999 for the 11-inch and $1,299 for the 12.9-inch.

The iPad Pro M3 will be available for pre-order starting today, with availability beginning next week on April 28, 2025. The Apple Pencil Pro is priced at $149, and the new Magic Keyboard starts at $299 for the 11-inch model and $349 for the 12.9-inch model.

Bottom Line

The iPad Pro M3 represents a significant evolution in Apple's professional tablet lineup. The combination of the powerful M3 chip, stunning OLED display, and enhanced accessories creates a compelling package for creative professionals, power users, and anyone who demands the best in mobile computing.

With its improved performance, display technology, and integration with professional software tools, the iPad Pro M3 continues to blur the line between tablets and traditional computers, offering a versatile and powerful computing experience in a sleek and portable form factor.
