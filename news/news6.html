Apple Glass Unveiled

The future of augmented reality is here

Published: April 12, 2025
By Apple News Team

In a groundbreaking event at Apple Park today, Apple CEO Tim Cook unveiled the company's most anticipated product in years: Apple Glass. The sleek augmented reality glasses represent Apple's bold vision for the future of computing, promising to blend digital information seamlessly with the physical world.

"Today marks the beginning of a new era," Cook announced to thunderous applause from the audience. "Apple Glass will fundamentally change the way we interact with technology, with each other, and with the world around us."

Revolutionary Design

Apple Glass features a minimalist design that looks remarkably like standard eyewear. Available in several styles and colors, the lightweight frames house advanced technology while maintaining a sleek profile. The lenses themselves are transparent displays that can overlay digital information onto the user's view of the real world.

Apple's head of design explained that creating glasses that people would actually want to wear was the team's biggest challenge. "We spent years refining the design to ensure Apple Glass is comfortable for all-day wear while still packing in all the technology needed for a truly immersive AR experience."

"This is the most personal device we've ever created. Apple Glass doesn't just change what you see—it changes how you see the world."

— Tim Cook, Apple CEO

Powerful Technology

Powered by Apple's new R1 spatial computing chip, Apple Glass delivers responsive AR experiences with incredible visual fidelity. The glasses feature advanced eye-tracking technology, gesture recognition, and spatial audio to create truly immersive experiences.

RealityOS

Apple Glass runs on RealityOS, a new operating system designed specifically for spatial computing. The interface is controlled through a combination of eye movements, hand gestures, and voice commands, eliminating the need for traditional input devices.

Craig Federighi, Apple's senior vice president of Software Engineering, demonstrated how apps appear as floating windows that can be arranged in the user's field of view. "RealityOS makes digital content feel like a natural part of your environment," he explained. "It's intuitive, responsive, and designed for the way humans naturally interact with the world."

App Ecosystem

Apple announced that over 1,000 AR apps will be available for Apple Glass at launch. Major developers showcased impressive demos, including:

  • AR Maps: Apple's own mapping application that overlays directions and points of interest onto the real world.
  • Spatial Notes: Leave virtual sticky notes attached to real-world locations.
  • FaceTime Holograms: See 3D representations of callers in your physical space.
  • Workspace: Transform any location into a virtual workspace with multiple screens.
  • Immersive Games: Several game developers demonstrated titles that blend digital characters with real-world environments.

Apple also announced a $100 million developer fund to encourage innovative AR applications for Apple Glass.

Pricing and Availability

Apple Glass will be available for pre-order starting today, with in-store availability beginning May 15th. The standard model starts at $1,999, with premium finishes and prescription lens options available at additional cost.

The company also announced Apple Glass Studio, an in-store experience where customers can get personalized fittings. "We want to ensure everyone gets the perfect fit," said Apple's retail chief. "This is a deeply personal device, and the experience needs to be just as personal."

Industry analysts are already predicting that Apple Glass will revolutionize the AR market, with projected sales of 10 million units in the first year. The device represents Apple's first major new product category since the Apple Watch, and early reactions suggest it could be even more transformative.

For those wondering about the future of iPhone, Apple executives were quick to note that Apple Glass is designed to complement the iPhone, not replace it. The glasses can pair with iPhone for additional functionality, though they also work independently for many tasks.

With today's announcement, Apple has once again redefined what's possible with consumer technology. Whether Apple Glass will fulfill its promise to usher in a new era of spatial computing remains to be seen, but one thing is clear: the future of augmented reality has arrived, and it looks remarkably like a pair of glasses.
